About Us

Mission

The Overcoming Adversity Cariño Scholarship Foundation is devoted to uplifting Latinx students who are excluded from federal aid, offering financial support and heartfelt mentorship. Our mission is to break cycles of generational poverty, open doors to opportunity, and guide students toward building generational wealth—creating brighter futures not just for themselves, but for their families and communities.

Vision

Break generational cycles of poverty by empowering Latino students and families through education, creating pathways to opportunity and lasting change.

Founder

Jonnathan Yepez Cariño is a visionary leader and community advocate dedicated to empowering others, particularly within the Latinx community.

As the Founder of the Overcoming Adversity Cariño Scholarship, he champions educational opportunities for underserved students. He serves as CEO of Sin Fronteras Consulting LLC, offering strategic guidance to businesses and nonprofits. Jonnathan is VP Sr. Branch Manager for Pacific Premier Bank, driving financial inclusion and growth. Passionate about supporting his Latinx community, he works to ensure access to tools and resources for building generational wealth. He also leads as Co-President of the Greater Marysville Tulalip Chamber Foundation, and member of the Snohomish Community Foundation’s Finance Committee and board.

Board of Directors

Jaime Méndez, an Emmy-winning journalist and influencer, is a pioneer of Spanish-language media in Washington. With nearly 30 years in communications, he was the lead news anchor for Univision Seattle since its launch in 2007.

In 2024, he launched Se Habla Media, where he leads as CEO, bringing independent journalism to social media. A passionate advocate for youth and education, Jaime supports the Overcoming Adversity Cariño Scholarship Foundation to empower students.

Originally from Colombia, he moved to the U.S. in 1987 and earned his degree in Communications and Social Sciences from The Evergreen State College.

Laurie Barbosa, the daughter of Mexican and Costa Rican immigrants, has served over 20 years as an Engineering Coordinator for the City of Marysville. A passionate advocate for social justice, she champions housing, education, mental health, and food security in Snohomish County.

Graduating Magna Cum Laude with a 4.0 GPA from Western Washington University, Laurie is now pursuing a master’s in social work to expand her impact. She supports students through the Overcoming Adversity Cariño Scholarship Foundation and serves on multiple boards, including Millennia Ministries and the City of Marysville’s DEIAB Advisory Committee.

Committed to equity and inclusion, Laurie works tirelessly to build a stronger, more just community.

Griselda Guevara-Cruz, the daughter of Mixtecan immigrants, is a trilingual first-generation professional currently serving as a Program Supervisor at the Washington Office of Superintendent of Public Instruction.

She comes from a farmworker family who spent many years traveling between Washington state, Oregon, and California. Passionate about advocating for and creating equitable access to educational opportunities for young people, Griselda has dedicated her career to advancing postsecondary opportunities for students in Washington state.

Griselda is a proud graduate of Mariner High School, Whitman College (BA), The University of Texas at Austin (MA), and the Congressional Hispanic Caucus Institute. She is a featured author in the Harvard Kennedy School Journal of Hispanic Policy, Vol. 30 (“The Importance of College-Going Culture for Latinos Prior to High School”). Griselda is also the recipient of the 2012 National Association for Chicana and Chicano Studies Frederick A. Cervantes Undergraduate Student Premio for her research contribution to Chicana/o Studies.

She currently serves as a Trustee on the Sno-Isle Libraries Board of Trustees and an application reviewer for a national organization’s fellowship programs focused on leadership development. Griselda looks forward to contributing her expertise to the goals of the Overcoming Adversity Cariño Scholarship Foundation.
